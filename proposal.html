The Proposal

Will You Have Dessert With Me?
 
Day 1 - June 8, 2012
I spent all day Friday flying from Seattle to Toronto partly because of the time zone difference. I had been craving some authentic Peking Duck as it's one of my favourite dishes. Joanne told me there was a fantastic place in the Pacific Mall plaza, so I asked her to invite her aunt and uncle to join my family for dinner that night.

During dinner, I excused myself to get a camera to take pictures. This wasn't unusual as we both take pictures of good food we eat, but also as a keepsake to remember the gathering. After taking a few photos, I handed the camera to my sister to continue shooting.

At that time, I asked for everyone's attention to announce there was a burning question on my mind. Joanne's face turned red as she knew a proposal was coming. She actually tried to stop me as she didn't want it to be that restaurant. We had reservations later that week at much nicer places.

I began by thanking all the guests for attending because it was close family for both of us. I also recounted the several years we've known each other and how much we care for each other. Everyone was scrambling to record the occassion and prepare for the eventual outcome. I proceeded to get down on one knee and nervously utter the words, "Will you have dessert with me?"

Thus began the seven days of proposals.
 
Day 2 - June 9, 2012
The second day was a lot of fun, because it involved a whole team helping me. I had messaged Joanne's best friend Jun before my arrival to setup a special dinner with some close friends, so we could have a night out at a fancy restaurant and dress nicely. The venue was the Fifth Grill in the heart of downtown. Definitely a romantic spot for a date.

As soon as we got to the restaurant, Joanne wanted to take a look around and check out the terrace. I, instead went right to the waitress and asked her to do a little favour for me after we finished the main course. When we sat down at the table, I discreetly passed a few folded up notes to Jun to distribute around the table.

When it came time to clear dishes away, the waitress conveniently came over to take a group photo with Joanne and I at the forefront. As the count slowly approached three, her friends all whipped out their sheets of paper. Joanne turned around to read the hidden message, "Will you have dessert with me?"

Second yes, 2/2!
 
Day 3 - June 10, 2012
No trip to Toronto is complete without going to Niagara Falls. Since my aunt from Singapore was visiting, we decided to take a trip to see one of the seven natural wonders of the world. It was my first time taking the Maid of the Mist, so I took every opportunity to take in the sights and do what I came to do.

As we circled the horseshoe falls and the water was raining on top of us, I asked my mother to take the camera and start snapping photos of us. With the background in our postcardesque picture, I leaned over to Joanne and whispered in her ear, "Will you have dessert with me tonight during the fireworks?"

As a Hilton Gold member, I booked a suite at the Fallviews Casino Resort and received a complimentary upgrade with a view better than Skylon Tower. The fireworks started like clockwork at 9pm. As they were winding down, Joanne leaned over to me and whispered back, "Yes!"
 
Day 4 - June 11, 2012
Another case of the Mondays. Joanne went back to work that day. By the time she left the office, it was raining. We wanted to grab a quick bite, so we went to Ben Thanh to get something quick and cheap.

During the course of dinner, I casually pulled out five balloons one-by-one. As I blew them up, all Joanne could do was try to stop me. After all, we had reservations booked for her favourite restaurant the next night and she had picked out a special dress. Slowly, but surely, I spelled out:

"Joanne"
"I love you."
"Will
"you..."
"have dessert with me?"

With several onlookers at the restaurant congratulating us, there's no way we couldn't have dessert after that meal.
 
Day 5 - June 12, 2012
The next day we went to a restaurant called Verses. It's called that because the restaurant itself was converted from a church and has the beautiful ambiance to match. Joanne has been there many times with friends, so has gotten to know the owner over time. This definitely helped, as I was able to make use of the staff to capture the moment.

As usual, dinner was fantastic. The setting really was romantic enough to be the moment to pop the big question, and I'm sure Joanne had that thought on her mind. Not many patrons were dining, so it literally as if the entire restaurant was reserved for us.

After our main course, Joanne excused herself to the powder room, while I set my plan in motion. As she walked back into the room, I was ready with my cue cards. Think Love Actually, but with a paparazzi entourage.

"We've known each other a very long time."
"And we've eaten at some amazing restaurants."
"And had some amazing views."
"But none compare to what I see every time we're together."
"I see a lifetime of happiness."
"I see us growing old."
"So I have one question for you.

Will you..."
"have dessert with me?"
 
Day 6 - June 13, 2012
We spent the day showing my aunt around downtown Toronto; mostly looking around St. Lawrence Market. As we eventually walked into Chinatown, we looked for a restaurant to sit down and eat. We couldn't exactly decide on a place, since Chinatown is not what it use to be. Joanne and I had a slight disagreement on where we should eat. I wanted to go somewhere fancy, yet she kept trying to Yelp for the highest ratings. Little did she know there was a reason I was very insistent.

Dinner wasn't that bad, but service could have been better. I had to play it up to pretend I was upset with her, but it was all part of the plan! I didn't even speak to Joanne the whole ride home. She must have thought something really bad was going to happen.

When we got home, she pulled me aside to ask me point blank why I was unhappy. With a serious look, I told her that tonight was the night. All my hard work and planning was foiled because we didn't have the right atmosphere. I even took out a box to show my sincerity. On it was simply "Tiffany & Co."

Joanne started to tear up. I handed the box slowly to her as I bent down on one knee. I opened it up and inside was a shiny new...iPod shuffle. On it was a collection of 50 of her favourite love songs. She almost smacked me when she realised she wasn't getting a ring that night. Luckily, she found it adorable there was an engraving on the back that read, "Will you have dessert with me?"
 
Day 7 - June 14, 2012
After six nights of fun, I had to keep her guessing, so instead of doing the same routine and fooling her into another proposal, the plan was to do nothing!

This really caught her off-guard. She kept asking me all night,
"Are you sure there's no dessert tonight?"
"Are you sure there is no surprise?"
"How come there is no dessert tonight?"
"How come we're not going for dessert?"
I simply kept replying with a big grin, "what dessert?"

Even as she dozed off, she kept expecting something big to happen. Luckily, the best was yet to come.
 
Day 8 - June 15, 2012
Finally the big day had come. I planned for a romantic day of walking around Toronto and hitting up some of my old hangouts. It was my show, so Joanne never questioned where I was leading her. We ended up taking a very leisurely walk around the city and even ended up at a Tiffany's to take a look at rings. As daylight faded, we got ready for dinner.

We had reservations at my favourite restaurant Canoe. It's a hidden gem at the top of the city overlooking both the CN Tower and SkyDome; a place we both enjoyed during Summerlicious a couple years back. Last time we went, Joanne was disappointed with the lack of a view, since they wouldn't seat us near the window. This time I came prepared.

Of course I had made special arrangements for a table for two, but what I didn't expect was to get the best seat in the house. The staff even brought in a round table, so we could sit beside each other instead of across from me other. We felt like royalty. They knew it was a significant night for us, since I had brought in a surprise for dessert.

As conditioned, when dinner was over, Joanne had stepped away to powder her nose. When she came back, I was simply waiting for her. She seemed puzzled as she tried to guess what I would do next. The maître d' came over to take our dessert order, so we made a couple of selections. When he came back with dessert, the whole room was staring in anticipation.

On a giant plate, six special cupcakes were carefully placed to spell out:
"Joanne"
"Will"
"You"
"Marry"
"Me?"
The last cupcake held the ring. The wait was finally over.
